What is Malaria?

Malaria is a severe febrile illness caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Plasmodium. It is not a virus or bacteria, but a complex organism that attacks the red blood cells.

Without prompt diagnosis and effective treatment, malaria can progress to severe disease and death, particularly in children and pregnant women. Our AI aims to democratize access to high-speed screening.

Essential Components

Understanding how malaria spreads and recognizing early warning signs.

Anopheles Vector

Mosquito Bite (Anopheles)

Primary transmission. Female mosquitoes inject Plasmodium parasites into the bloodstream when feeding on human blood.

Parasite Life Cycle

Plasmodium parasites first infect the liver, then enter red blood cells, multiplying and causing symptoms. Understanding the cycle helps in prevention and treatment.

🚨

Severe Risks

Untreated malaria can lead to organ failure, anemia, cerebral malaria, and can be life-threatening.

Other Modes of Transmission

Besides mosquito bites, malaria can spread through other rare pathways.

💉

Blood Transfusion

In rare cases, malaria can be transmitted through transfusions with infected blood or organ transplants.

🤰

Mother to Child

Known as congenital malaria. An infected mother can pass the parasite to her baby before or during birth.

🩸

Contaminated Needles

Sharing needles for injections or tattoos with infected blood can occasionally transmit malaria.

Common Symptoms

Symptoms typically appear 10–15 days after the infective bite.

🤒

High Fever

Sudden high fever that can spike up to 104°F (40°C), often accompanied by sweating and body aches.

🥶

Shaking Chills

Intense shivering and chills, often alternating with hot sweats, indicating the body’s reaction to the parasite.

🧠

Severe Headache

Persistent, throbbing headaches, usually accompanied by fatigue and sensitivity to light.

🤢

Vomiting

Nausea and vomiting due to fever and infection, which can lead to dehydration if untreated.
